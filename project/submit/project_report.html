Conclusion

  1. Experiment-4 gives the best results. It is from tuning the hyper parameters 'num_leaves': ag.space.Int(lower=52, upper=132, default=72),  # number of leaves in trees (integer hyperparameter).

  1. Also additional features such as datetime to categorizes morning, lunch, and evening rush hour, such as 7-9am, 11am-1pm, and 5-6pm, and Feature that categorizes hot/cold/mild temps from temp, feature that categories very windy/mild from wind, and feature that categories very humid/ not from humid have improved the R2 scores and kaggle score significant from the raw model.

  1. Changing the parameters num_bag_folds=10, num_bag_sets=1, num_stack_levels=3 also improved the R2 score and kaggle score but the training time requirements increased significantly.

  1. Lasty, NOT all hyper parameter changes helps the performance,  changing 'num_boost_round': 200,num_trials = 10,time_limit = 15*60" actually decreases the performance and the kaggle score.
